--- DOCUMENT 1: Employee Leave Policy ---

Employees are entitled to the following types of leave each calendar year:

Annual Leave:
Employees receive 20 paid annual leave days per year. Annual leave must be
requested through the internal HR portal at least 3 days in advance and is
subject to manager approval.

Sick Leave:
Employees are entitled to 12 sick leave days per year. If an employee is sick
for more than two consecutive days, a medical certificate may be required.

Emergency Leave:
Emergency leave may be granted for urgent personal matters such as family
medical emergencies or critical personal situations. Approval is determined
by the employee's manager and HR department.

Unused annual leave can be carried forward for up to 6 months into the next
calendar year unless otherwise specified by HR.

--- DOCUMENT 2: Expense Reimbursement Policy ---

Employees may request reimbursement for approved business-related expenses.

Eligible expenses include:
• Travel expenses for work-related trips
• Accommodation for business travel
• Client meeting expenses
• Office supplies purchased for work purposes

To apply for reimbursement:

1. Log into the internal Expense Management Portal.
2. Submit the reimbursement request form.
3. Upload all required receipts or invoices.
4. Provide a short description of the expense purpose.

Once submitted, the finance team reviews the request. Approved reimbursements
are typically processed within 5–7 business days and transferred to the
employee’s registered bank account.

Expenses submitted more than 30 days after the purchase date may not be eligible
for reimbursement unless approved by the finance department.

--- DOCUMENT 3: Remote Work Policy ---

The company supports flexible and remote work arrangements.

Employees may work remotely up to 3 days per week unless their role requires
onsite presence.

Guidelines for remote work include:

• Employees must remain reachable via company communication tools during
  standard working hours.
• All remote employees must attend scheduled team meetings.
• Employees must ensure secure access to company systems when working remotely.
• Confidential company information must not be accessed from unsecured devices.

Employees wishing to work fully remote must submit a request to their manager
and HR department for approval.

--- DOCUMENT 4: IT Support and Help Desk ---

Employees experiencing technical issues should contact the IT support team.

Common IT support requests include:

• Password resets
• Software installation requests
• Hardware troubleshooting
• Network connectivity issues
• Access permission requests

Employees can contact IT support using the following methods:

1. Submit a ticket through the IT Help Desk Portal.
2. Email the support team at: support@company.com
3. Use the internal messaging channel “#it-support”.

IT support requests are typically addressed within 24 hours during business days.

For urgent issues such as system outages, employees should mark the support
ticket as “High Priority”.

--- DOCUMENT 5: Employee Onboarding Guidelines ---

New employees must complete the onboarding process within their first week.

The onboarding process includes:

• Setting up company email and communication accounts
• Completing HR documentation
• Reviewing company policies and code of conduct
• Attending the onboarding orientation session
• Meeting with team members and assigned mentor

The HR department provides onboarding materials and training resources to help
new employees become familiar with company tools, workflows, and expectations.

Managers are responsible for guiding new employees through their first projects
and ensuring they understand team responsibilities.

--- DOCUMENT 6: Performance Review Process ---

Employee performance reviews are conducted twice per year.

The review process includes:

• Self-assessment submitted by the employee
• Manager performance evaluation
• Feedback on achievements and areas for improvement
• Goal setting for the next review cycle

Performance reviews help ensure employees receive clear feedback, career
development support, and recognition for their contributions.

Employees who consistently exceed expectations may be considered for promotions
or additional responsibilities.
